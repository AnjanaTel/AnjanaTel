# Hi, I'm Anjana 👋

I'm an astrophysics PhD candidate at the **University of Minnesota** 
working at the intersection of X-ray and Very-High-Energy (VHE; E > 100 GeV) 
gamma-ray astronomy. My research focuses on understanding how supermassive 
black holes launch and power relativistic jets — using the universe as my 
laboratory and some of the most sensitive telescopes on Earth and in space 
as my tools.

I work on multiwavelength observational campaigns, combining data across 
optical, X-ray, and gamma-ray wavelengths to build a complete physical picture 
of AGN jet behavior during flaring events in radio galaxies. Radio galaxies 
are rare VHE emitters — their jets are only weakly Doppler boosted toward us, 
making them intrinsically difficult to detect at these energies and restricting 
detections to nearby sources. Yet these same sources have produced some of the 
most extreme VHE flares on record, making them fascinating laboratories for 
testing jet physics and particle acceleration and emission scenarios.

A significant part of my work involves gamma-ray data analysis with VERITAS, 
including retraining Boosted Decision Trees (BDTs) for gamma-hadron separation 
for a full season of data. This involved working closely with both simulations 
and real data, optimizing BDT score cuts on known AGN sources, and validating 
the retrained models on the Crab Nebula as a standard candle. Improved BDT 
performance directly enhances sensitivity to weak gamma-ray emitters — exactly 
the kind of sources my science targets. I'm a member of the **VERITAS 
Collaboration** and have presented my work at international conferences 
including **ICRC 2023** in Nagoya and **HEPRO**.

In my research, I work across the full data pipeline — from raw telescope 
data to spectral modeling to publication-ready results — and I care deeply 
about communicating science lucidly and contributing meaningfully.
